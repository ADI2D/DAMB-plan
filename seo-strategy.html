MASTER LANDING PAGE SPECIFICATIONS

Purpose: Primary SEO and conversion page for each franchise territory. Must be the strongest page in the territory.

URL Format: /locations/[state]/[city]/

Required Sections:

  1. Hero Section
    • H1: "Adaptive Fitness in [City], [State]"
    • Subheadline addressing caregiver pain point
    • CTA: "Schedule Your Free Intro Session"
    • Trust badges (certifications, years in operation)
  2. What We Do
    • Service explanation with outcomes focus
    • Who we serve (populations)
    • What makes us different
  3. Local Proof Section
    • Nearby landmarks, schools, hospitals
    • Cities/areas served
    • Local community involvement
  4. E-E-A-T Section
    • Owner/trainer background and qualifications
    • Relevant certifications (ACSM, NSCA CSPS, Adaptive Training Academy AIT)
    • Background check statement
    • Athletes served count, years in operation
  5. Session Expectations
    • What happens in Free Intro Session
    • Duration (e.g., 30-45 minutes)
    • Whether caregivers can join
    • No commitment required
  6. Testimonials
    • 3-5 testimonials minimum
    • Include BOTH caregiver AND participant perspectives
    • Video testimonials preferred
    • Google reviews embed
  7. Caregiver-Focused Section
    • Directly address caregiver concerns
    • Safety and privacy assurances
    • How program supports both athlete and caregiver
  8. FAQ Section
    • Age limitations
    • HSA eligibility
    • Contract/commitment terms
    • Safety protocols
    • Privacy policy
  9. Final CTA
    • Repeat Free Intro Session offer
    • 30-day guarantee mention
    • Phone number and form

TAB 4: TIER 1 CITY CRITERIA

Definition

A Tier 1 city qualifies for its own dedicated page. Cities that don't meet criteria are mentioned on the state pillar page or nearest Tier 1 city page—they do NOT get standalone pages.

Qualification Criteria

A city must meet AT LEAST 3 of the following 5 criteria:

#CriterionThresholdHow to Verify
1Population25,000+Census data
2Household IncomeAbove state medianCensus/demographic data
3Name RecognitionStrong regional awarenessJudgment call
4Hub StatusHub city for surrounding townsGeographic analysis
5Special Needs EcosystemTherapy centers, hospitals, special edLocal research

Why This Matters

Building pages for cities that don't meet Tier 1 criteria:

  • Creates thin content (bad for SEO)
  • Dilutes crawl budget
  • Causes keyword cannibalization
  • Increases maintenance burden
  • Provides no meaningful traffic

Rule: If it's not Tier 1, it doesn't get a page.

TAB 5: E-E-A-T REQUIREMENTS

What is E-E-A-T?

E-E-A-T is Google's framework for evaluating content quality:

  • Experience: Does the creator have first-hand experience with the topic?
  • Expertise: Does the creator have the knowledge/skill in this area?
  • Authoritativeness: Is the creator/site a recognized authority?
  • Trustworthiness: Is the content accurate, honest, safe, and reliable?

Why It Matters for Special Strong

Special Strong serves a vulnerable population. Google applies higher scrutiny to "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL) topics. Without strong E-E-A-T signals, pages will struggle to rank regardless of technical optimization.

TAB 6: CONVERSION OPTIMIZATION

Primary CTA: Free Stronger You Intro Session

Why "Intro Session" vs "Trial":

  • "Trial" implies evaluation/judgment
  • "Intro Session" is exploratory and welcoming
  • Lower psychological barrier for caregivers

CTA Clarification: Include what happens next: "A 30-minute session to discuss your athlete's goals and see our approach firsthand. No commitment required."

Pricing Strategy

ApproachEffect
✗ "Pricing determined after first session"Creates hesitation, feels like sales trap
✓ "Sessions starting at $X"Anchors expectation, reduces friction
✓ "HSA-eligible"Removes financial barrier
✓ "No long-term contracts"Reduces commitment fear

Caregiver-Focused Messaging

The buyer is typically a parent or caregiver. Address their concerns directly:

Safety Section:

  • "All trainers pass comprehensive background checks"
  • "Certified in [specific certifications]"
  • "Trained specifically for adaptive populations"

Session Expectations Section:

  • What happens during the intro session
  • Session duration
  • "Caregivers welcome to observe"
  • No pressure, no commitment

Privacy Section:

  • "Your information is protected"
  • Link to privacy policy

Caregiver Support Section:

  • Acknowledge the caregiver journey
  • Explain respite/support benefits
  • "We support the whole family"

Testimonial Strategy

Must include BOTH perspectives:

  • Caregiver testimonials (the decision-maker)
  • Participant testimonials (the beneficiary)
  • Video preferred over text

TAB 8: MIGRATION PLAN

Phase 1: Pre-Migration Audit (Week 1-2)

Google Search Console Audit:

  • ☐ Verify all local franchise domains in GSC
  • ☐ Export indexed URLs from each domain
  • ☐ Identify pages with existing backlinks
  • ☐ Document top-performing pages by traffic
  • ☐ Review coverage reports for each property

Traffic Analysis:

  • ☐ Pull 90-day traffic data per page
  • ☐ Flag pages with >100 sessions/month for manual review
  • ☐ Document conversion data per page if available

Phase 2: Pre-Launch Setup (Week 2-3)

  • ☐ State pillar pages created and reviewed
  • ☐ Master landing pages created per template
  • ☐ City cluster pages created (Tier 1 only)
  • ☐ All schema markup implemented
  • ☐ All pages pass Core Web Vitals
  • ☐ 301 redirect rules written (not yet deployed)

Phase 3: Redirect Execution (Week 3-4)

Deployment Sequence:

  1. Deploy all new pages to production
  2. Submit new sitemaps to GSC
  3. Request indexing for priority pages
  4. Wait for initial indexing (2-5 days)
  5. Implement 301 redirects (server-level)
  6. Test every redirect manually
  7. Monitor GSC for errors
  8. Remove old pages only after redirect confirmation

Redirect Rules:

  • All redirects must be 301 (permanent)
  • Server-level implementation required
  • No redirect chains (A→B, not A→B→C)
  • No registrar-level forwarding

Phase 4: Post-Migration Monitoring (Week 4-8)

  • ☐ Daily GSC monitoring (Week 1-2)
  • ☐ Watch for indexing drops
  • ☐ Validate Google replaced old URLs with new
  • ☐ Monthly traffic comparison
  • ☐ Quarterly backlink audit
